Notivox

A tool that turns a recording into music you can edit.

Notivox takes an audio or video file, pulls out the main melody, and turns it into notation you can actually work with — transpose it, adapt it to another instrument, and export a clean score. It started as a way to transcribe my grandfather’s cello playing: the slides and phrasing that a plain .wav file flattens into a list of numbers.

What it does

  • Transcribe — upload audio/video and extract the lead melody as notation.
  • Transpose — shift the melody to any key.
  • Re-voice — adapt it for a different instrument (piano, guitar, flute…).
  • Two modes — a rule-based pass for predictable cleanup, or an AI mode using pitch-tracking models for trickier audio.
  • Export — download the cleaned-up score as PDF or MIDI.

Generate & interact

Beyond transcription, Notivox can generate music from a plain-language prompt (“a jazzy line in D minor”) and render it as sheet music. You can also interact with a melody you uploaded — for example, “write a harmony using only I–IV–V chords for this melody.”

Under the hood

Audio processing in Python (LibROSA, Essentia) with music analysis via music21; AI transcription using pitch-tracking models (CREPE / SPICE) and generation through the OpenAI API; notation rendered with LilyPond / ABCjs and exported to PDF and MIDI.
